Australia is most at risk!

From the map, we can see that the country who spends the most amount of money per capita on gambling, is Australia. This means that in Australia, minors are at a higher risk of being exposed to gambling compared other countries around the world.

This type of gambling activity will leads to young adults being falsely mislead into believing that gambling may be a regular hobby or past time, however, the reality is far from it.



What types of gambling?

The chart above indicates that gambling machines by far contribute the net loss. What may seem like an innocent $5 play may and often does turn into an uncontrollable addiction. In conjunection with the fact that most of these machines have bright, colourful cartoons and animations for every spin, and the results are catastrophic.

The chart above also depicts wagering that is another primary contributer to the net losses of Australia. Wagering is most commonly called sports betting or horse betting. Much like gambling machines, minors stand a high chance of being exposed to sports/horse betting advertisements when they themselves watch sport as they commonly appear during breaks.



Third section

The line chart indicates that the states of New South Wales, Victoria and Queensland contribute the most to the total gambling net loss of Australia. This indicates that whilst not the risk of children and teenagers being exposed to gambling in these states is much higher than others, but also indicates that these states contain many individuals who suffer from gambling addication and financial difficulties.

The chart also indicate fairly steep drop in net losses in 2010 compared to previous years for New South Wales. This is due to the new gambling regulations that were introduced in 2010. These regulations were updated from the previous one in 2002 and some change includes A ban on the use of credit cards to gamble at casinos and licensed clubs, A requirement for casinos to implement responsible gambling programs, including measures to identify and assist problem gamblers and Increased penalties for illegal gambling. These changes attempt to mitigate the impacts of long-term gambling addiction and from the chart, it worked. Whilst this may have temporaily mitigated net losses, they numbers have started to rise again due to factors such as increasing population, most worryingly, additiction is hard to resolve.
